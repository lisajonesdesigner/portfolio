User Flows and Wireframes

Welcome!

This course will teach you how designers map user journeys, evaluate onboarding experiences, and translate flows into low-fidelity wireframes.

There are three sessions that together form a blended 6-hour learning experience:

  • Session 1: Foundations of User Flows (in-person)
  • Session 2: Heuristics and Competitor Analysis (online live)
  • Session 3: From Flows to Wireframes (self-paced)

Each session contains readings and videos, guided activities, and some simple assessments that you will submit.

Learning Objectives

On successful completion of this unit, you should be able to:

  1. Explain and apply user-flow principles to model user journeys within digital products.
  2. Evaluate onboarding experiences using usability heuristics and competitor analysis.
  3. Design and annotate low-fidelity wireframes that demonstrate clear structure, logic, and accessibility.

About this unit

Subject matter: User Flows and Wireframes — designing intuitive experiences for digital products.

Who it’s for: Adult UX learners — early-career designers and professionals upskilling in human-centred and interface design. Cohort of 20–25 with mixed digital experience and strong motivation for applied learning.

How the unit is delivered

Session 1Foundations of User FlowsIn-person workshop · 2 hours
Session 2Heuristics and Competitor AnalysisSynchronous online (Zoom) · 2 hours
Session 3From Flows to WireframesSelf-paced online · 2 hours

Assessment

One project-based artefact submission — annotated wireframes based on a defined user-flow scenario. The task integrates all three sessions into one authentic UX workflow: model a user flow, analyse competitors and heuristics, then translate findings into annotated wireframes.

What you will do

  1. Read the LMS content for Sessions 1–3 (user flows, heuristics, wireframing).
  2. Develop a complete onboarding flow for the fictional NSURE insurance app.
  3. Conduct a competitor analysis (two direct + two indirect) using the LMS template.
  4. Create four to five annotated low-fidelity wireframes visualising the journey and referencing heuristics.
  5. Complete the auto-graded LMS quiz on wireframe principles (≥ 80%).
  6. Upload final artefacts (PDF or images) and quiz certificate to the LMS portal.

Marking criteria

  • Accuracy and clarity of user-flow logic (LO1).
  • Application of heuristics and competitive insight (LO2).
  • Completeness and annotation quality of wireframes (LO3).
  • Visual clarity and professional presentation with captions or alt-text.
  • Adherence to submission instructions.

Submission

Upload to the LMS assessment portal by 11:59 PM at the end of the study period. Individual submission; collaboration is welcome during practice activities.
