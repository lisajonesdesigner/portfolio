Session 2: Analysis and Wireframes

Session Overview

During this session, you examine usability principles, heuristics, and evaluation methods used in digital products. You learn how to identify strengths and weaknesses in onboarding experiences through competitor analysis. You continue building your NSURE onboarding concepts by refining user flows and developing early wireframes. Two activities form the upload tasks for this session.

2.1 Usability and Competitor Analysis

2.1 Usability and Competitor Analysis

Topic Overview

Usability focuses on how easy a product is to use. Good usability reduces effort, errors, and frustration. In this topic, you explore usability principles, the role of Nielsen’s 10 heuristics, and how competitor analysis helps design teams understand the broader market environment.

You review direct and indirect competitors and learn how to examine onboarding patterns across products. You complete this topic with a competitor analysis activity that evaluates onboarding flows related to the NSURE case study.

2.2 User Flows and Wireframes

2.2 User Flows and Wireframes

Topic Overview

This topic bridges user flows and early interface design. You explore how flows support iteration and how wireframes translate ideas into structure. Wireframes show content placement, actions, and hierarchy without focusing on visuals.

You review iteration, layout logic, and wireframe techniques. You finish with an activity that requires sketching paper wireframes for the NSURE onboarding flow.
